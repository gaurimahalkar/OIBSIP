Lata Mangeshkar

The Melody Queen of India

A Voice That Lives Forever

Lata Mangeshkar was one of the greatest playback singers in India. Her melodious voice became the identity of Indian cinema for more than seven decades. She inspired millions through her dedication, humility and unforgettable songs.

What I Adore

Her songs continue to bring joy, comfort, and unforgettable memories to people around the world.

About Lata Mangeshkar

Lata Mangeshkar was born on 28 September 1929 in Indore, Madhya Pradesh. She was the eldest daughter of Pandit Deenanath Mangeshkar, a renowned classical singer and theatre artist. From a young age, she received training in classical music from her father.

After the death of her father in 1942, she started singing professionally to support her family. Her hard work, discipline and exceptional talent helped her become the most respected playback singer in India. During her career she recorded thousands of songs in more than thirty languages and worked with almost every legendary music director in the Indian film industry.

Career Journey

Her breakthrough came with the song Aayega Aanewala from the movie Mahal (1949). From then onwards, she became the voice of many famous Bollywood actresses. She collaborated with legendary composers like Naushad, S. D. Burman, R. D. Burman, Madan Mohan, Laxmikant-Pyarelal and A. R. Rahman.

Her singing career lasted for more than seventy years, making her one of the longest-serving and most respected artists in Indian music history.
